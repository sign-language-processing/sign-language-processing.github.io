# Sign Language Translation using Skip Connections and Selective Self-transfer Learning

**Year:** [[2025]]
**Topics:** [[topics/computer-science|Computer Science]], [[topics/linguistics|Linguistics]]
**Authors:** Takeshi Kajiyama; Tomoya Murakami; Taro Miyazaki; Hiroyuki Kaneko

## Abstract

Machine translation from a sign language to another language is expected to help in communication between deaf people and hearing individuals. Due to the absence of a writing system in sign languages, it is difficult to use text-based machine translation directly for sign language. Therefore, research is underway to analyze visual features extracted from video instead of text and translate them into other languages. In deep learning-based sign language translation, CNNs are typically used as extractors of visual features, which play a crucial role in translation performance. However, difficulty in training the extractor has become a bottleneck. We propose a method that focuses on the length of the backpropagation path, one of the factors that makes training challenging. By introducing a skip connection inside a translation network to shorten the path, the bottleneck in the training of the extractor is mitigated. To further enhance performance, we also propose a pre-training method for the extractor. To address local minima and overfitting in the downstream layers of the network, we re-initialize the parameters in these layers while retaining those of the extractor in the upstream layers, enabling training to resume with pre-trained parameters. The pre-training does not rely on either costly annotation of gloss or dedicated pre-training networks, which incur optimization and training cost. We conducted experiments confirming the effectiveness of the proposed methods and show that the best results can be achieved using both methods together.
