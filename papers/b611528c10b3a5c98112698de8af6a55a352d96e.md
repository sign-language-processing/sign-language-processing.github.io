# Generalizable Sign Language Recognition via Local Temporal Convolutions and Region-Aware Pose Encoding

## Abstract

Continuous Sign Language Recognition (CSLR) aims to translate sign language videos into gloss-level annotations. While RGB-based models leveraging global sequence models have shown strong performance, they often face challenges in generalizing to unseen sentence compositions due to their reliance on visual appearance and long-range context modeling. In this work, we present a lightweight poseonly CSLR framework that focuses on improving robustness in the unseen sentence setting, where sentence compositions at test time differ from those observed during training. Our approach uses pose keypoints as the sole input modality to reduce visual redundancy and background noise. We explore a locally conditioned decoding strategy based on temporal convolution to mitigate potential overfitting associated with global sequence models. Through empirical analysis on a large-scale signer-independent benchmark, we find that our method achieves competitive performance compared to published RGB-based baselines, despite relying solely on 2D pose input. These results suggest that pose-driven representations, combined with localized temporal modeling and multilevel supervision, offer a promising and efficient direction for building robust CSLR systems.
