## Abstract

. We conduct an initial investigation to gain insight into whether a deep neural network learns phonological aspects of sign language when classifying video recordings of isolated signs from a continuous signing scenario. We train a series of neural networks to distinguish pairs of signs in Dutch Sign Language, controlling the phonological difference between the signs in each pair. Our results suggest that the intrinsic di-mension of the final hidden layer of a network is surprisingly insensitive to the phonological difference between the signs in a pair. However, the ability of the network to discriminate two signs shows a clear trend towards increasing with increasing phonological distinctiveness.
