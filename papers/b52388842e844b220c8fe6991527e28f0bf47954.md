# Deep Learning-Based Sign Translation of Indian Sign Language for Educational and Technical Vocabulary

**Topics:** [[topics/computer-science|Computer Science]], [[topics/education|Education]], [[topics/linguistics|Linguistics]]
**Authors:** G. Chethana; M. N. Pushpalatha

## Abstract

Members of the deaf and hard-of-hearing community rely on sign language as their primary and essential form of communication because it provides them with a rich system of visual gestures and expressions that allow them to express complex ideas, and thoughts. But there is still a significant communication gap between signers and the general public, mostly because non-signers don’t know much about sign language. This paper presents a novel, gloss-free Indian Sign Language (ISL) translation system that directly converts 2 D sign language videos into expressive 3D avatar animations and synthesis speech output, bypassing traditional intermediate representations like gloss or text. The proposed system combines deep convolutional and recurrent neural networks for spatial-temporal feature extraction, and leverages DeepMotion for realistic 3D avatar animation. This module uses a mix of metrics-Mean Squared Error (MSE), Structural Similarity Index (SSIM), Peak Signal-to-Noise Ratio (PSNR), Cosine Similarity, and Dynamic Time Warping (DTW)-to assess how accurately the output reflects the original signs. The proposed system demonstrated high semantic fidelity and visual accuracy, achieving SSIM scores above 0.85 and Cosine Similarity consistently above 0.95. While structural and perceptual similarities remained stable, some variability was observed in temporal alignment, indicating challenges in gesture pacing during complex sequences. Designed with the needs of the deaf and hard-of-hearing community in mind, the system bridges a key accessibility gap by focusing on technical and instructional vocabulary in Indian Sign Language (ISL). Overall, this work contributes to the advancement of inclusive communication technologies by offering a multimodal, scalable solution for sign language translation.
