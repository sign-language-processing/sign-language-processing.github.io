# Combining cross-domain adapter and cross-frame sparse aggregation for continuous sign language recognition

**Authors:** Li He; Guangqian Kong; Xun Duan; Huiyun Long

## Abstract

Abstract. Continuous sign language recognition (CSLR) is a vital research field in computer vision, aimed at bridging the communication gap between the deaf and hard of hearing community and the hearing world. However, adapting powerful pretrained vision-language models such as contrastive language-image pretraining (CLIP) to CSLR is challenging due to two main gaps: a domain gap between general visual concepts and sign language semantics, and a temporal gap caused by the model’s frame-independent design, which fails to capture motion dynamics across frames. To address these issues, we propose SignCLIP, a unified CSLR framework that integrates two key modules: a cross-domain adapter (CDA) and a cross-frame sparse aggregation (CFSA). Our approach employs a frozen CLIP visual backbone to preserve its rich pretrained knowledge. The CDA bridges the domain gap by incorporating global temporal enhancement and local temporal difference modeling. Conversely, the CFSA tackles the temporal gap by effectively capturing human motion trajectories and inter-frame correlations. The integration of CDA and CFSA yields complementary advantages: the domain-specific features refined by the CDA provide a robust foundation for the CFSA to model temporal dynamics, whereas the motion patterns captured by the CFSA, in turn, enhance the domain adaptation process. This synergy enables SignCLIP to address both challenges concurrently and efficiently, forming a unified and parameter-efficient framework that retains general knowledge while excelling in the sign language domain. Extensive experiments have shown that, compared with existing methods, SignCLIP achieves competitive results on three publicly available CSLR datasets: PHOENIX-2014, PHOENIX-2014-T, and CSL-Daily.
