# Exploring Sign-level Strategies to Enhance Automatic Translation of French Sign Language

## Abstract

This work addresses the automatic translation of sign language videos into text, using a state-of-the-art transformer architecture that combines autoregressive inference with CTC-based classification. Due to the difficulties in manually annotating existing sign language video datasets, we explore two methods for automatic labeling: (1) using an annotator trained on a sign-text dictionary to automatically label unseen videos, (2) generating pseudo-sign labels through unsupervised clustering. Both approaches achieve an improvement in BLEURT score compared to a baseline transformer-based encoder-decoder model on the French Sign Language dataset Mediapi-RGB. We also evaluate the generalization capability of the proposed methods on German and British Sign Language datasets.
