# Hybrid feature based deep ensemble approach for Indian sign language recognition

**Year:** [[2025]]
**Topics:** [[topics/physics|Physics]], [[topics/computer-science|Computer Science]], [[topics/linguistics|Linguistics]]
**Authors:** Soumen Das; S. K. Biswas; B. Purkayastha

## Abstract

The Sign Language Recognition System (SLRS) represents an advanced technology designed to improve accessibility in communication within the deaf community, replacing the conventional method of relying on human translators. However, the current SLR systems for Indian Sign Language (ISL) suffer from inefficiency and a lack of robustness due to their reliance on a single classifier. To address these limitations, this research paper introduces a robust and efficient SLRS named Hybrid Deep Ensemble Network (HD-ENet1) for recognizing ISL words. The proposed HD-ENet1 incorporates a technique for selecting keyframes based on histogram differences which effectively eliminates redundant frames. To enhance system performance and ensure robustness, the method leverages a weighted average ensemble approach, utilizing three distinct models: LSTM, ConvLSTM, and BiLSTM networks as base classifiers. In order to determine the optimal combination of weights, the HD-ENet1 integrates the Hill Climbing (HC) optimization technique. The performance of the HD-ENet1 is measured with repeated k-fold cross validation technique where the proposed system demonstrated an average classification accuracy of 97.32% on the Indian Sign Language (ISL) dataset and 98.78% on the benchmark LSA64 dataset. A comparative analysis is conducted with several SLRS, revealing that the suggested approach consistently outperformed the existing approaches.
