# Bridging Communication Gaps with Bidirectional Italian Sign Language Translation Adapting ASR Technology

**Year:** [[2025]]
**Topics:** [[topics/computer-science|Computer Science]], [[topics/linguistics|Linguistics]]
**Authors:** Nicolas Tagliabue; Elisa Colletti; Roberto Tedesco; Francesco Roberto Dani; Alessandro Trivilini; Sonia Cenceschi

## Abstract

DEEP is a bidirectional translation system between Italian Sign Language (LIS) and Italian, tailored for pharmacies and municipal registry interactions with a dedicated dataset. The system features two independent pipelines enabling chat-like interaction: deaf users sign through a camera with a virtual LIS interpreter, while hearing users interact through a chat interface. The deaf UI operates fully automatically without manual input to start/stop conversations. LIS-to-Italian translation uses a customized Whisper model processing body measure converted into “pseudo spectrograms,” achieving BLEU 61.84 and BLEURT 0.81. Italian-to-LIS uses pre-recorded sentences performed by a photo realistic human avatar. Testing showed 73.4% of LIS translations were comprehensible, 97.2% of Italian-to-LIS translations were clear enough, and 92.6% of sentences were correctly managed by the automatic start/stop mechanism. A key contribution is the DEEP dataset, composed of ~62 hours annotated LIS videos.
