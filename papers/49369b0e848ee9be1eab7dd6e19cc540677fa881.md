## Abstract

American Sign Language (ASL) is a visual language with unique syntactic and grammatical structures. Translating ASL into coherent English text is critical for accessibility and inclusion, yet remains challenging due to limited labeled datasets, irregular syntax, and domain-specific vocabulary. This paper presents a complete pipeline for ASL-to-English transformation using the T5 Transformer model, guided by real-world ASL transcription data. A custom web crawling process is implemented to extract structured ASLEnglish sentence pairs from accessible online Sentence-Piece based platforms involved tokenization, stop word filtering, noise reduction, and data augmentation using NLP techniques. A specialized vocabulary was designed to handle frequent ASL ngrams and was integrated into the tokenizer to better preserve linguistic nuances. T5 fine-tuning was performed using the Hugging-Face Transformers library in a GPU-accelerated environment. Model outputs were evaluation scores with BLEU and ROUGE metrics against traditional Seq2Seq baselines. We also used attention heatmaps to visualize decoder focus during translation. The proposed method significantly improves fluency, syntactic clarity and contextual meaning in ASL- to-English translation.
