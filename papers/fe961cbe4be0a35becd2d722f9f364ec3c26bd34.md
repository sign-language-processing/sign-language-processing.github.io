# Computer-based tracking, analysis, and visualization of linguistically significant nonmanual events in American Sign Language (ASL)

**Year:** [[2014]]
**Topics:** [[topics/computer-science|Computer Science]], [[topics/linguistics|Linguistics]]
**Authors:** C. Neidle; Jingjing Liu; Bo Liu; Xi Peng; Christian Vogler; Dimitris N. Metaxas

## Links
- [Semantic Scholar](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/fe961cbe4be0a35becd2d722f9f364ec3c26bd34)

## TL;DR

A linguistically motivated computational approach and the results for detection and recognition of nonmanual grammatical markings are summarized and demonstrated; and work underway to enable such visualizations to be produced over large corpora and shared publicly on the Web is described.
