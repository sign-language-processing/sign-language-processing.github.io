# Emotive Audio Synthesis for Sign Language Translation

**Topics:** [[topics/computer-science|Computer Science]], [[topics/linguistics|Linguistics]]
**Authors:** Ashwina Rakish; Anirudh Taawker; A. Patil; Arya Rushipathak; M. Begam

## Abstract

Sign recognition and sign translation are still research areas of active interest to computer vision. Conventional sign language translation algorithms translate sign language videos into gloss, an intermediate representation of sign. They are thus limited to processing gloss-annotated datasets, which are normally small since gloss annotation is cumbersome and done manually. Modern methods usually ignore the emotions and facial expressions of the signer, which can affect the quality of conversation. This paper suggests a novel process that solves both problems by directly interpreting sign language into translated text and generating emotive audio. The process uses an adapted version of Camgoz et al.'s joint end-to-end transformer-based sign language to text translation process, adapted to function without gloss annotations. Trained with a subset of 10,000 front-facing How2Sign clips (about 13k vocabulary), the translation model scored BLEU-1 to BLEU-4 of 16.67, 7.23, 3.19, and 1.32. These are close to the ones obtained by prior work, but working on a smaller set with a smaller video-to-vocabulary ratio. These results provide promising potential in interplaying direct translation and multimodal emotion detection to yield human-like expressive speech from sign language videos.
