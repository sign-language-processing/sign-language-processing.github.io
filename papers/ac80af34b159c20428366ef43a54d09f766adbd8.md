# Hybrid System for Macedonian Sign Language Translation and Recognition Using NLP, Computer Vision and 3D Animation

## Abstract

Macedonian Sign Language (MSL) remains underrepresented in digital technologies, creating significant barriers in education and communication. This paper presents a multimodal system that integrates linguistic processing, computer vision, and 3D animation to provide both automatic translation from Macedonian text into sign language and realtime recognition of signs. The pipeline consists of three major modules. The first module is a hybrid text processing system that combines grammatical rules with a fine-tuned multilingual transformer (mBART-50) to convert Macedonian sentences into glosses, achieving a BLEU score of 30.1 and a chrF of 45.0 after hybrid normalization. The second module focuses on visualization, using MediaPipe to extract keypoints from sign videos and Blender to render these into fluid 3D avatar animations. The third module performs recognition of the Macedonian manual alphabet using MobileNetV2 trained on an augmented dataset of over 38,000 images. The system achieves 97.2% validation accuracy and a macro F1-score of 0.936 in recognition tasks. The modular design makes the system suitable for educational platforms, accessibility services, and further research into continuous sign translation.
